Mall Miles

This UX design case study was a challege to design a mobile app that solved an everyday problem. Mall Miles is a collaboration between four UX designers who split the ideation, research, design, and iterative processes evenly and worked on the entire project as a team. The goal was to create a mid-fidelity prototype of the app.

Team Members: Ella Bodle, Gideon Lee, Tara Farzin, Abraham Marquez

May 24 - June 16, 2021

Tools: Miro, Figma, Invision, Google Suite, Zoom

​
​ ​ ​ ​ ​

the problem

​

Malls can be tough to navigate - plus dealing with lines and parking difficulties add to the stress of shopping.

the solution

Mall Miles was developed as a modern day directory and navigation solution for both people who seldom visit the mall and for mall rats alike. Mall Miles easily navigates you to your stores and allows you to report and view line wait times, parking, and busy areas - all while earning rewards!

​ ​ ​
Through our research, we noticed that modern shoppers can be impatient and feel frustrated by the mall shopping experience, hate wasting time navigating a mall and have low motivation. ​ And Mall Miles was born. Mall Miles was designed to help any shopper easily navigate any mall all while earning rewards.
​ ​ ​ ​
​

the process

​
​ ​

As user researchers, we wanted to: Discover how mall shoppers spend their time navigating through the mall; understand how customers make their shopping decisions. , and discover how customers decide what stores to go to first. ​ We did this through creating a proto persona, conducting user interviews with quesitons like "What are some frustrations you face when shopping at the mall? ". We then analyzed the insights from our interviews into insight statments, affinity diagrams, empathy maps, and created a problem statement to focus on. ​ Additional Methods: ​ Tools: Figma, Miro, Zoom, Invision

low-fidelity protype, testing, & iteration

​

Each team member individually created a hand drawn, low-fidelity wireframe based off of the user flow.

The wireframes were then converted into a clickable low-fidelity prototype. We each presented our wireframes within the team, demonstrating the functionality. Through this, we were able to make note of different layouts and design choices and usability. We decided on one low-fidelity prototype that best fit all of our ideas and user flows and tested it for usability.

​

Our insights from testing and watching users navigate our wireframe allowed us to make valuable changes to our mid-fidelity prototype

​
​ ​

mid-fidelity prototype, testing, & iteration

​
​

Each team member created a medium- fidelity prototype based off the combination of ideas from the low-fidelity prototypes and testing insights.

This allowed different features and usability to be viewed in various ways to determine efficacy.

​ Our team reviewed each prototype and noted limitations and possible improvements. From there we collaborated on our ideas and iterated on our initial design to improve our design. ​

takeaways & the future

We provided a unique solution by limiting our scope and niche to focus on a specific need of our users. We wanted to provide an app that helps to streamline the shopping experience, and ultimately make the shopping process fun in 2021 ​ The amount of time we had when completing the app was an obstacle in our process. We were also unable to test Mall Miles in an actual mall with real users because of covid related restrictions. We plan to continue to iterate on our design with further user testing. Our next phase of development would be to build out additional functionality including a QR function and additional functionality of the navigation and rewards portion of the app. We plan to further refine the UI elements and create a high-fidelity prototype.
